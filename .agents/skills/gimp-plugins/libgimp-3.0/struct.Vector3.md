###### [gimp\_vector3\_add](type_func.Vector3.add.html)

Computes the sum of two 3D vectors. The resulting `GimpVector3` is
stored in `result`.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_sub](type_func.Vector3.sub.html)

#### Instance methods

###### [gimp\_vector3\_add\_val](method.Vector3.add_val.html)

This function is identical to `gimp_vector3_add()` but the vectors
are passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_cross\_product](method.Vector3.cross_product.html)

Compute the cross product of two vectors. The result is a
`GimpVector3` which is orthogonal to both `vector1` and `vector2`. If
`vector1` and `vector2` and parallel, the result will be the nul vector.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_cross\_product\_val](method.Vector3.cross_product_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_cross_product()`, but the
vectors are passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_inner\_product](method.Vector3.inner_product.html)

Computes the inner (dot) product of two 3D vectors. This product
is zero if and only if the two vectors are orthogonal.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_inner\_product\_val](method.Vector3.inner_product_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_inner_product()`, but the
vectors are passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_length](method.Vector3.length.html)

Computes the length of a 3D vector.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_length\_val](method.Vector3.length_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_length()`, but the vector
is passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_mul](method.Vector3.mul.html)

Multiplies each component of the `vector` by `factor`. Note that
this is equivalent to multiplying the vectors length by `factor`.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_mul\_val](method.Vector3.mul_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_mul()`, but the vector is
passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_neg](method.Vector3.neg.html)

Negates the `vector` (i.e. negate all its coordinates).

###### [gimp\_vector3\_neg\_val](method.Vector3.neg_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_neg()`, but the vector
is passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_normalize](method.Vector3.normalize.html)

Normalizes the `vector` so the length of the `vector` is 1.0. The nul
vector will not be changed.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_normalize\_val](method.Vector3.normalize_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_normalize()`, but the
vector is passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_rotate](method.Vector3.rotate.html)

Rotates the `vector` around the three axis (Z, Y, and X) by `alpha`,
`beta` and `gamma`, respectively.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_rotate\_val](method.Vector3.rotate_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector3_rotate()`, but the vectors
are passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_set](method.Vector3.set.html)

Sets the X, Y and Z coordinates of `vector` to `x`, `y` and `z`.

###### [gimp\_vector3\_sub\_val](method.Vector3.sub_val.html)

This function is identical to `gimp_vector3_sub()` but the vectors
are passed by value rather than by reference.
