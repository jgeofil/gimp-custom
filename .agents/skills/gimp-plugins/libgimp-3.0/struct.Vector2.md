###### [gimp\_vector2\_add\_val](method.Vector2.add_val.html)

###### [gimp\_vector2\_cross\_product](method.Vector2.cross_product.html)

Compute the cross product of two vectors. The result is a
`GimpVector2` which is orthogonal to both `vector1` and `vector2`. If
`vector1` and `vector2` are parallel, the result will be the nul vector.

###### [gimp\_vector2\_cross\_product\_val](method.Vector2.cross_product_val.html)

Identical to `gimp_vector2_cross_product()`, but the
vectors are passed by value rather than by reference.

###### [gimp\_vector2\_inner\_product](method.Vector2.inner_product.html)

Computes the inner (dot) product of two 2D vectors.
This product is zero if and only if the two vectors are orthogonal.

###### [gimp\_vector2\_inner\_product\_val](method.Vector2.inner_product_val.html)

vectors are passed by value rather than by reference.
